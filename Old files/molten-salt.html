Molten Salt SMRs

Coolant & Moderator

MSRs use molten halide salts as primary coolant, operating at high temperatures (600–900°C) while maintaining near-atmospheric pressure. This eliminates high-pressure accident risks. These salts are chemically stable and provide strong negative temperature feedback. Designs may use graphite moderators or operate in fast neutron spectra.

Fuel & Core Design

Fuel can be liquid (dissolved in salt) or solid (TRISO particles). Liquid-fueled designs allow for online refueling and continuous removal of gaseous fission products, suppressing xenon poisoning. Heat is transferred via three loops: a radioactive primary salt loop, a clean intermediate salt loop, and a tertiary power conversion loop.

Safety Features

  • Freeze Plug: A salt section that melts during overheating, draining fuel by gravity into subcritical tanks.
  • Low Pressure: Operates at atmospheric pressure, reducing structural mechanical stress.
  • Passive Cooling: Decay heat is removed via natural convection and radiation.

Operation & Transients

MSRs exhibit self-regulating behavior due to strong negative reactivity coefficients. Continuous stripping of gases like Xenon and Krypton maintains stable reactivity. However, salt chemistry requires precise control to manage corrosion and prevent salt freezing in compact configurations.

Technical Analysis

Advantages

  • High Efficiency: Can exceed 50% thermal efficiency.
  • Non-Flammable: No risk of hydrogen explosions or sodium fires.
  • Resource Utilization: Can recycle actinides and use thorium/uranium fertile loads.
  • Excellent Load Following: Quickly adjusts to grid demand variations.
  • Compact/Modular: Core volume is used efficiently for decentralized power.

Disadvantages

  • Material Corrosion: High temperatures increase structural degradation.
  • Tritium Management: Requires specialized containment systems.
  • Complex Processing: Online fuel processing is not yet commercially proven.
  • Regulatory Hurdles: Lack of historical operational experience.
  • Waste Forms: Standardized disposal forms for MSR fuel are still in development.
