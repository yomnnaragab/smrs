Introduction

The accelerating global demand for clean, secure, and reliable energy has renewed focus on nuclear power as a vital component of the low-carbon transition. With a growing population and industrial development, the world's electricity demand continues to rise.

Renewable energy sources, while essential, remain intermittent and cannot alone provide stable baseload power. Consequently, nuclear energy is increasingly recognized as indispensable to achieving international climate goals.

Conventional large-scale nuclear power plants face persistent challenges, including high capital costs, complex supply chains, and extended construction timelines. These limitations have motivated a global shift toward Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) as a promising solution.

Small
