Liquid Metal SMRs

Coolant Selection

LM-SMRs use Sodium (Na), Lead (Pb), or Lead-Bismuth Eutectic (LBE) to achieve high thermal efficiency and low-pressure operation. These metals offer thermal conductivity significantly higher than water, enabling rapid heat extraction.

Fast Reactor Core

LM-SMRs typically operate as fast neutron reactors (0.1–1 MeV) without moderators. Fuel assemblies are arranged in hexagonal lattices using UO2 (15–20% enrichment), MOX, or metallic fuels (U-Zr). Metallic fuels offer superior conductivity and breeding potential, reaching burnups of 80–100 GWd/t.

Safety Mechanisms

  • Passive Decay Heat: Natural circulation removes up to 10% of nominal power without pumps.
  • Inherent Feedback: Relies on Doppler broadening and coolant-density effects.
  • Atmospheric Pressure: Operates at 0.1–0.2 MPa, minimizing mechanical stress.

Durability & Control

Lead and LBE require strict oxygen concentration control to prevent steel corrosion. Sodium systems utilize double-walled piping and inert cover gas to manage chemical reactivity with air and water. Cladding uses advanced HT-9 ferritic–martensitic steel.

Technical Analysis

Advantages

  • High Power Density: Up to 100 MW/m³ for compact, transportable units.
  • Sustainability: Breeding ratios of 1.1–1.2 produce additional fissile material.
  • Operational Flexibility: High heat capacity supports rapid load-following.
  • Long Life: Refueling intervals of 3–5 years reduce maintenance downtime.

Disadvantages

  • Corrosion: Selective leaching and grain-boundary penetration of materials.
  • Toxicity: LBE produces Polonium-210, requiring strict radiological protection.
  • Structural Loads: High coolant density increases seismic and support requirements.
  • Reactivity: Sodium reacts violently with water and air.
