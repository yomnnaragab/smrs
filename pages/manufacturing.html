Manufacturing

Key Components & Fabrication

Reactor Core & Nuclear Fuel

Fuel fabrication begins with uranium dioxide powder, which is pressed into pellets, sintered, and loaded into zirconium alloy cladding to form fuel rods. These rods are assembled into fuel bundles that make up the reactor core, requiring extremely precise geometry to ensure safe heat removal, neutron moderation, and long-term operation.

Fuel types depend on reactor design. Although LEU and HALEU fabrication processes are similar, HALEU requires stricter criticality controls and specialized licensing. Some advanced SMRs use TRISO fuel, consisting of coated spherical fuel particles that provide exceptional fission product containment and undergo rigorous quality assurance, making them among the safest fuel designs.

The Reactor Pressure Vessel (RPV)

The reactor pressure vessel is manufactured from high-strength low-alloy steel using advanced forging, machining, and heat treatment processes. Automated welding techniques ensure high-quality joints with minimal defects.

Extensive non-destructive testing and hydrostatic pressure tests verify vessel integrity. The smaller size of SMR RPVs simplifies fabrication, transportation, and installation, making them more adaptable to factory-based production environments.

Control Rods

Control rods regulate reactor power by absorbing neutrons. They are manufactured using absorber materials such as boron carbide, silver–indium–cadmium alloys, or hafnium, enclosed in metallic cladding.

The completed rods undergo rigorous mechanical, thermal, and vibration testing to ensure maximum reliability during reactor operation, acting as the primary system for both power adjustment and emergency shutdown.

Steam Generators

Steam generators transfer heat from the reactor to the power cycle and are manufactured from thousands of high-alloy tubes housed within pressure vessels.

Advanced manufacturing techniques—including automated welding, high-precision leak detection, and rigorous pressure testing—ensure safe and efficient heat transfer. This high-integrity construction prevents cross-contamination between the primary and secondary coolant circuits.

Instrumentation & Control Systems

SMR instrumentation and control systems are fully digital and factory-tested under extensive fault and operating scenarios. This rigorous testing ensures that the system can handle a wide variety of real-world conditions before it ever leaves the factory.

Once validated, the software is finalized and delivered as an integrated control module. This represents a significant advancement over traditional analog nuclear control systems, offering enhanced precision, automated safety logic, and cyber-secure architectures.
