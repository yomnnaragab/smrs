NuScale Power Module™

A scalable pressurized water reactor design from the United States.

Project Overview

The NuScale Power Module™ is a light-water-cooled SMR PWR with scalable plant configurations. The standard plant offers up to 12 modules, each producing 77 MWe (gross), for a total plant output of 924 MWe. Each factory-fabricated, self-contained module operates independently and is managed from a single control room. The design employs natural circulation cooling, eliminating the need for reactor coolant pumps.

Design Philosophy & Safety

Simplicity: By placing the core, steam generators, and pressurizer inside a single integral vessel, large primary piping is eliminated, removing LOCA risks.

Passive Safety: The design relies on physics (gravity, convection) rather than electricity. The Triple Crown of Safety™ ensures indefinite cooling without operator action, AC/DC power, or additional water.

Resilience: Modules are submerged in a large reactor pool that serves as an ultimate heat sink for up to 150 days.

Fuel & Waste Strategy

  • Fuel: Standard UO₂ assemblies (17x17) with < 4.95% enrichment.
  • Cycle: 18-month refueling cycle with "in-out" shuffle scheme.
  • Storage: Up to 15 years in cooling pools followed by on-site dry cask storage for the plant's 60-year life.

Target Applications

• Baseload Electricity
• Load-Following Renewables
• District Heating
• Hydrogen Production
• Desalination
• Coal Plant Repowering

Key Stats

Power: 77 MWe per module

Type: Integral ipWR

Coolant: Light Water

Circulation: Natural

Status: US NRC Design Approval (2023)

HTR-PM

The world's first commercial High-Temperature Gas-Cooled Reactor.

Project Overview

Developed by Tsinghua University, the HTR-PM (High-Temperature Reactor-Pebble-bed Modules) connects two 250 MWt reactor modules to a single 210 MWe steam turbine. It represents the transition from the experimental HTR-10 to a commercial-scale demonstration plant.

Status: Connected to the grid in Dec 2021; Commercial operation began Dec 2023.

Design Philosophy & Safety

Inherent Safety: The design eliminates the possibility of core melt. The low power density and high thermal capacity of the graphite moderator ensure peak temperatures stay below 1600°C even during total loss of cooling.

TRISO Fuel: Encapsulates fission products within ceramic layers effectively containing radiation at the source.

Passive Cooling: Decay heat is removed via the Reactor Cavity Cooling System (RCCS) through natural processes (conduction/radiation).

Fuel Cycle Strategy

  • Fuel Format: Spherical "Pebbles" (60mm diameter) containing TRISO particles.
  • Refueling: Continuous on-line refueling. Fresh pebbles are added to the top, and spent pebbles are discharged from the bottom.
  • Cycle: "Once-through" cycle. Pebbles pass through the core multiple times (~15) until target burnup is reached.

Target Applications

• Efficient Power Generation
• High-Temperature Process Heat
• Hydrogen Production
• District Heating

Waste Management

The HTR-PM waste management strategy uses automated handling and long-term on-site dry storage for spent fuel. Spent fuel is stored in sealed, shielded tanks with passive cooling ensured by natural ventilation, even under accident conditions. Final disposal is planned in a national geological repository.

Key Stats

Power: 210 MWe (2 modules)

Temp: 750°C Outlet

Coolant: Helium Gas

Moderator: Graphite

Location: Shidao Bay, China
