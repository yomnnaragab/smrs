NuScale Power Module™

Case Study I • United States of America

Overview

The NuScale Power Module™ is a light-water-cooled SMR PWR with scalable plant configurations of 4 modules (308 MWₑ), 6 modules (462 MWₑ), or 12 modules (924 MWₑ), with the 6-module plant as the licensing reference. Each factory-fabricated, self-contained module operates independently and is managed from a single control room, with NRC approval for three operators to oversee up to 12 modules. The design relies on integrated containment, natural circulation cooling, high-pressure containment, and proven light-water reactor technology.

Target Applications

1. Electricity Generation

Standard baseload power supply.

2. Flexible Grid Operation

Load following for renewables.

3. Non-Electrical Heat

Industrial heat & desalination.

4. Coal Repowering

Transitioning coal-fired sites.

Design Philosophy

The NuScale SMR design philosophy focuses on simplicity, modularity, and passive safety while relying on proven light-water reactor technology. It uses a conventional PWR core with 17×17 UO₂ fuel assemblies, enrichment up to 4.95%, and B₄C control rods.

The integral NSSS design places the core, helical-coil steam generators, and pressurizer inside a single reactor pressure vessel, eliminating external primary piping and coolant pumps and enabling natural circulation cooling.

Safety is ensured through fully passive systems that require no power or operator action. A 60-year design life, 18-month refueling cycle, and staggered module refueling support high-availability operation.

Safety Features

The NuScale NPM uses a fully passive safety design that ensures indefinite shutdown and self-cooling without power, operator action, or added water. Safety relies on an integral primary system and steel containment immersed in a reactor pool.

Decay heat is removed by a natural-circulation DHRS with passive condensers. The below-grade containment and reactor pool provide a long-term passive heat sink, allowing up to 150 days of passive cooling after total power loss.

Licensing Status

May 2025: NRC granted a Standard Design Approval (SDA) for the uprated 77 MWe module, completing the technical review ahead of schedule.

Fuel Cycle Approach

Fuel Type and Enrichment:

The NPM utilizes uranium dioxide (UO2) fuel. The U-235 enrichment is below the U.S. manufacturer’s limit of 4.95 percent enrichment.

Refueling:

Three-batch refueling is conducted on a nominal 18-month cycle in an “in-out” shuffle scheme. One-third of fuel assemblies are removed and placed in the spent fuel pool.

Waste Management

Includes below-grade pool storage for initial cooling for up to 15 years, followed by on-site dry-cask storage. The layout accommodates dry storage for all spent fuel generated over the 60-year lifetime.

A dedicated radioactive waste building processes gaseous, liquid, and solid wastes using volume reduction and HEPA-filtered systems to minimize exposure.
